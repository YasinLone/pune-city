welcome to sinhagad

Sinhagad Fort, also known as Sinhgarh Fort, is a historic fortress located atop a hill in the Bhuleshwar Range of the Sahyadri Mountains, near Pune, Maharashtra, India.
The name "Sinhagad" translates to "Lion's Fort" in Marathi. The fort holds immense historical significance and has been the site of several important battles throughout its existence. Originally built around 2000 years ago, Sinhagad Fort has undergone numerous renovations and expansions over the centuries under different rulers, including the Marathas, Mughals, and the British. The fort was strategically important due to its commanding position overlooking the surrounding landscape, making it a formidable stronghold. One of the most famous battles associated with Sinhagad Fort is the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where Tanaji Malusare, a Maratha warrior, led a daring nighttime assault to recapture the fort from the Mughals.
Tanaji's heroic sacrifice during the battle is celebrated in Marathi folklore and history.
Today, Sinhagad Fort is a popular tourist destination, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and Pune city.
Visitors can reach the fort by trekking or driving up the winding road that leads to its entrance. The fort complex includes several notable structures such as gates, bastions, temples, and the memorial of Tanaji Malusare.
Apart from its historical and scenic attractions, Sinhagad Fort is also renowned for its natural beauty, especially during the monsoon season when the surrounding landscape is lush and verdant. The trek to the fort is a favorite activity for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers, providing an opportunity to explore the region's rich heritage and picturesque surroundings. Overall, Sinhagad Fort stands as a testament to the valor of its defenders and the enduring legacy of the Maratha Empire, while also serving as a popular destination for tourists seeking adventure, history, and breathtaking views.
